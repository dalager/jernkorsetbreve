# Peter Mærsk's Military Service (1913-1918)

**Unit:** Infanterie-Regiment General-Feldmarschall v. Hindenburg (2. Masurisches) Nr. 147
**Parent formation:** 73. Infanterie-Brigade, 37. Division, XX. Armeekorps
**Peacetime garrison:** Lötzen (Giżycko), East Prussia

---

## Identity

Peter Mærsk (also: J. P. Marsk Roagger, Peter Gad) was a Danish-speaking farmer from Roagger (Roager) parish, Øster Åbølling, in Sønderjylland (North Schleswig). The region was under German sovereignty (1864-1920), so Peter served in the German Imperial Army.

## Regiment: Infanterie-Regiment Nr. 147

IR 147 was a Prussian infantry regiment garrisoned in **Lötzen** and **Lyck** (now Giżycko and Ełk, Poland) in the Masurian lake district. The regiment received the honorary designation "General-Feldmarschall v. Hindenburg" after the Battle of Tannenberg (August 1914), which was fought in its home region.

The 37. Division was rated by Allied intelligence as **"a first class shock division"** — it saw continuous front-line service from first to last day of the war.

### 37. Division structure (1914)

```
XX. Armeekorps (Allenstein)
└── 37. Division (Allenstein)
    ├── 73. Infanterie-Brigade (Lyck)
    │   ├── Infanterie-Regiment Nr. 147 (Lötzen/Lyck)  ← Peter's regiment
    │   │   ├── I. Bataillon (1.-4. Kompagni)
    │   │   ├── II. Bataillon (5.-8. Kompagni)
    │   │   └── III. Bataillon (9.-12. Kompagni)  ← Peter's battalion
    │   └── Infanterie-Regiment Nr. 151
    └── 74. Infanterie-Brigade
        ├── Infanterie-Regiment Nr. 146
        └── Infanterie-Regiment Nr. 150
```

Peter was initially assigned to **12. Kompagni** (III. Bataillon).

## Commanding Officers

### "Den Gamle (Majoren)" = Major Beerbohm

The letters' most frequently referenced military figure after Konow is **"den gamle"** (the old one), identified in letter 281 as "den gamle (Majoren)" and confirmed as **Major Beerbohm** in letter 274. He appears in 53+ letters.

Beerbohm was likely the commander of **III. Bataillon** (Peter's original battalion) or possibly the regimental commander during Peter's time at Reg. Stab. Peter served as his personal orderly from October 1915 onward, accompanying him on rounds through the trenches (letters 293, 296) and to Divisional headquarters.

## Implications for Person Registry (ADR-042/044)

### "Den Gamle (Majoren)" = Major Beerbohm

The person registry entry "Den Gamle (Majoren)" (53 letters, rank #5) can now be identified as **Major Beerbohm**. The registry should be updated to use this name. Beerbohm was likely Peter's direct superior at Reg. Stab from October 1915 onward.

The separate registry entry "Beerbohm" (5 letters) is the same person — these should be merged. Beerbohm's combined mention count would be approximately **58 letters**, placing him solidly at #5 in the network.
